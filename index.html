0–12 Months

First Sounds & Responses

  • Babies coo, gurgle, and react to familiar voices as they build trust through sound.

    Try this: Talk, sing, and respond to your baby’s sounds. Describe what you’re doing (“Now we’re changing your nappy”) to wrap actions in language.

  • Little ones begin experimenting with first words like mama or baba and start to understand simple phrases.

    Try this: Play peek-a-boo, name everyday objects, and imitate their babbles. Use gestures like waving bye-bye to connect words and actions.

12–24 Months

First Words & Two-Word Phrases

  • Toddlers label favourite people and objects and begin combining two words to share ideas.

    Try this: Label what your child points to, expand their words (“red ball”), and read picture books together while following their lead.

2–3 Years

Early Sentences & Questions

  • Children string together short sentences and explore the world with what and where questions.

    Try this: Ask open-ended questions, read stories, and talk about your day. Encourage curiosity—every question builds language.

3–4 Years

Storytelling & Imagination

  • Preschoolers tell simple stories and link ideas with connecting words like and or because.

    Try this: Ask what happened next or how characters felt. Dramatic play (shop, doctor, superhero) fuels new vocabulary.

4–6 Years

Reading Readiness & Narrative Skills

  • Children recognise letters, hear rhymes, and retell familiar stories as early reading takes shape.

    Try this: Read daily, play rhyming games, and invite them to invent stories. Celebrate attempts at writing and pretend reading.
